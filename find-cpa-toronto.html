Whether you need a CPA for personal tax filing, corporate tax planning, audit and assurance, or specialized services like forensic accounting or estate planning, Toronto has specialists in every area.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a CPA cost in Toronto?

Toronto CPA fees vary by service. Personal tax returns typically cost $150–$500 depending on complexity. Corporate tax returns range from $1,500–$5,000+. Bookkeeping services are often billed monthly at $500–$2,000/month. Audit and assurance engagements start at $5,000 for small businesses. Many Toronto CPAs offer free initial consultations.

What is the difference between a CPA and an accountant in Ontario?

In Ontario, CPA (Chartered Professional Accountant) is the only regulated accounting designation. CPAs are licensed by CPA Ontario and must meet ongoing education and ethics requirements. An accountant without CPA designation is not regulated and cannot sign audit reports, represent clients before the CRA in formal proceedings, or provide some attestation services.

What services do Toronto CPAs offer for small businesses?

Toronto CPAs serving small businesses typically offer corporate tax filing, HST returns, payroll services, bookkeeping, year-end financial statements, CRA audit support, business registration advice, and financial forecasting. Many offer bundled monthly packages for growing businesses.

How do I verify a CPA's credentials in Ontario?

You can verify any CPA's standing using the CPA Ontario member directory at cpao.ca. All CanadaAccountants profiles in Ontario are sourced from CPA Ontario registry data, so every listed professional has held or currently holds a valid Ontario CPA licence.
